Industrial Revolution Reference Material:
The Industrial Revolution was a period of global transition in the late 1700s and early 1800s. Key inventions like James Watt's improved steam engine revolutionized transport and manufacturing. Locomotives could now transport coal and textiles much faster than horse-drawn wagons. This led to a massive migration of workers from farmland to growing cities (urbanization) seeking employment in newly built factories. The factory system centralized labor, moving it away from the 'cottage industry' model, introducing rigid schedules and specialized tasks.
