Answer 1: Steam engines were very important because they powered the first trains, which we call iron horses. These trains could carry way more weight than a regular horse ever could. By putting these engines on tracks, companies could move coal and iron from the mountains down to the cities where the factories were. It made everything faster because you didn't have to wait for horses to rest or eat. This helped the whole country grow because goods could be sent to different towns much more easily than before when you had to use slow boats or carts on muddy roads.

Answer 2: Urban living became the new normal because that's where the money was. In the old days, everyone lived on a farm and grew their own food, but then the Industrial Revolution changed that. When factories started popping up in the cities, they needed people to run the big machines. Since farming was getting harder to make a living at, whole families moved to the city to find work. It was a big change because cities were crowded and dirty compared to the countryside, but it was necessary for people to survive and feed their families in the new economy.

Answer 3: The nature of labor changed because before people worked in a cottage industry. This meant they made things like shirts or tools in their own houses. But you can't fit a giant steam engine in a small house, so everyone had to go to a factory instead. At the factory, you couldn't just work whenever you felt like it. There were bosses and clocks that told you exactly when to start and when to stop. It made the work feel less like a craft and more like being a part of a big machine. Even though it was hard work, it allowed factories to make thousands of items at once instead of just one at a time at home.
