Answer 1: The impact of the steam engine was that it made the air very dirty because of all the smoke from the burning coal. It was a big machine that used boiling water to create pressure, and people used it for many things. One of those things was transportation. It helped boats go faster on the water and it helped trains go across the land. It was better than using animals because machines don't get tired, but they do need a lot of fuel to keep going. I think it made people travel more and see things they never saw before.

Answer 2: Urbanization is when people move to the city. During the Industrial Revolution, the cities were getting very big because of all the new buildings and factories being built every day. People liked living in the city more than the rural areas because there were more things to do like going to shops or meeting new people. Even though it was very crowded and sometimes there wasn't enough clean water, people still kept coming because they thought the city was the future. It changed how people lived because they didn't have as much space as they did on the farm, and they lived in small apartments instead of big houses.

Answer 3: Working in a factory was very different from working on a farm. On a farm, you work outside in the sun and you can see the sky and the trees. But in a factory, it is very dark and loud because of all the metal machines clanking together all day long. It was dangerous for the workers because they could get their hands caught in the gears if they weren't careful. The labor was hard because you had to stand in one place for a long time and do the same thing over and over again without any breaks. I think it made the workers very tired and sad because they didn't have any freedom.
