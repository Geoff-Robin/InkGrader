Answer 1: Transportation changed forever because of the steam engine. It was invented by James Watt and it allowed goods to be moved very quickly. Before this, people used horses and wagons which were slow and couldn't carry much. But once they had steam engines on locomotives, they could move tons of coal and textiles across the country in just a few days. This was a huge change for business and for people who wanted to buy things from far away. It was the start of the modern world of shipping and trade.

Answer 2: The Industrial Revolution made people move from the countryside to the city. This is what we call urbanization. It happened because the new factories were in the cities and they needed millions of workers to run the machines. People left their farms because they could make more money in the city, even though it was a very different kind of life. Instead of being surrounded by nature, they were surrounded by brick walls and other people. It was a complete shift in how human beings lived and worked together for the first time in history.

Answer 3: Labor changed because of the factory system. In the old days, people worked at home in a cottage industry, making things at their own pace. But in a factory, you have to work on a schedule and do exactly what the boss tells you to do. It was called division of labor because everyone had a small part of the job to do. This made production much faster, but it also meant the work was very boring for the workers. They had to follow the clock and work long hours in difficult conditions, which was very different from their old lives at home.
